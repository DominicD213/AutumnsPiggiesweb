Waffles

Waffles was adopted from a rehoming situation through someone I found on Facebook. He was originally advertised as a female, but once I took him home it was clear the owners had it wrong. He’s a very social and sweet guinea pig who loves to be pet like a dog. He’s also my oldest and largest pig, weighing a whopping 1.4kg.

Sage

Sage was adopted from my local SPCA to be a friend for Honey. She bonded wonderfully, and the two were together until Honey passed in May 2022. She has now been bonded to Stardust & Cinnamon. She's sweet and enjoys being pet, especially on her chin. When you pet her she tends to lay flat like a pancake.

Cinnamon

\

Cinnamon was adopted through someone I met here on Instagram. He’s a spunky & crazy boy with a lot of personality who likes to be held but not pet. He was originally adopted as a friend for Waffles but after their bond broke he was neutered and he bonded with some of my girls (Sage & Stardust).

Stardust

Stardust was adopted from a breeder who was rehoming all of her guinea pigs and shutting down her program. She is still quite shy, but very sweet. Stardust is a stain guinea pig, and sadly she has already developed satin syndrome (osteodystrophy) - a degenerative bone disease. She will be on medication for life. Despite this she is one of the most vocal guinea pigs of my group.

Rocket

Rocket was adopted through a local rescue to be a bar buddy for Waffles. He is a younger boy with a lot of spunk. He loves to hop & run around his cage, and for some reason, save his veggies to be eaten throughout the night. His Sheba fur is especially noticeable on his hind end, where it looks like he's wearing a little skirt.
